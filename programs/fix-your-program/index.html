The Problem

What happens when your coordinator leaves?

The average student affairs coordinator stays 2–3 years. School counselors: 3–4 years. Greek life advisors: 1–2 years. When they leave, they take the program with them — the intake forms they built, the matching spreadsheet only they understood, the session outline nobody else can find, and two years of institutional knowledge.

The new person walks in and faces a choice: rebuild from scratch (40+ hours they don't have) or let the program quietly fade out. This happens at almost every institution, on a 2–3 year cycle. It doesn't have to.
