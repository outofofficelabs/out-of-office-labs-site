The Access Gap

Most students who need mentorship
never get it.

Informal mentoring is not evenly distributed. Students with professional networks in their families get guidance as a matter of course. Students without those networks — first-generation students, rural students, students from under-resourced communities — are left to navigate alone. Structured programs close that gap. Without the structure, the gap widens.
